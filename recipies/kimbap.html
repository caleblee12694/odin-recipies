Kimbap

Kimbap is a traditional Korean dish that you would usually find in a school lunch box, as it is easily packed and enjoyed. Kimbap is very easy to customize with different ingredients, however it requires a lot of prep. Do not underestimate the prep time!

Ingredients List:

  • Seaweed (or Nori) sheets
  • Cooked Rice
  • Carrots
  • Korean Pickled Radish
  • Bulgogi (marinated beef)
  • Eggs
  • Spinach

Directions:

  1. Peel your carrots and cut them into matchstick-shaped pieces.
  2. Place carrots into a bowl with a generous amount of salt, and let them sweat out.
  3. Bring water up to a boil
  4. Blanch your spinach in the boiling water, making sure to squeeze out all of the water when they are done.
  5. Bring a frying pan up to medium heat
  6. While the frying pan is heating up, whisk 4 eggs and keep in a bowl nearby.
  7. Fry eggs gently onto a tablespoon of butter, and turn off the heat.
  8. Make sure the egg is very thin on the frying pan, and flip it once the bottom part is cooked.
  9. When done, move to a cutting board and cut into thin slices.
  10. Move finished eggs to a plate nearby (along with the spinach).
  11. Cut up the pickled radish into long pieces to put into the kimbap.
  12. Move sliced pickled radish onto the preparation plate.
  13. Heat up frying pan again with a splash of a neutral oil.
  14. Cook bulgogi onto frying pan until fully brown (with an internal temp of 165 degrees).
  15. If bulgogi is not sliced, slice into thin strips
  16. Move bulgogi to the preparation plate.
  17. Roast seaweed sheet over an open flame for about 5 seconds.
  18. Then, place onto a bamboo rolling mat.
  19. Place cooked rice onto the seaweed sheet and spread it out thinly.
  20. Then, place the carrots, spinach, eggs, and bulgogi onto the sheet horizontally.
  21. Roll it up tightly, and then brush with sesame oil.
  22. Sprinkle with sesame seeds, and then cut into bite sized pieces.
  23. Repeat as many times as you need.
  24. Enjoy your traditional Korean Kimbap!
