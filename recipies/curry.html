Japanese Curry Rice

Japanese Curry is a great dish if you are short on ingredients, or have assorted vegetables you need to get rid of. It differs from Indian curry by being a sweeter alternative, and uses less spices than Indian Curry.

Ingredients List:

  • Golden Curry Blocks (I prefer medium heat)
  • Yukon Gold or Russett Potatoes
  • Water
  • Onions
  • Carrots
  • Beef Chuck Roast

Directions:

  1. Start by heating up 500 ml of water up to a boil.
  2. Peel and cut potatoes into sizes of your choice, then put the potatoes in the water.
  3. Do the same to carrots, first peeling them and putting them in the water.
  4. Heat up a frying pan to medium-high and coat it with a neutral oil.
  5. Fry your onions until they become semi-transparent.
  6. Season your Beef Chuck Roast with a pinch of salt and a generous amount of pepper.
  7. Slice your Beef Chuck Roast into bite sized pieces, then put them on the frying pan.
  8. Fry them along with the onions until internal temperature has reached 165 degrees.
  9. Once you are done frying your onions and beef, turn off the heat and set them aside.
  10. Break up all of the cubes, putting 2 portions (8 cubes) into the water.
  11. Keep the water at about a medium heat, and mix cubes in until fully dissolved.
  12. Lastly, put in your meat and onions into the pot.
  13. Enjoy your curry over rice!
